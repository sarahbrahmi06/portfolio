Algérie Télécom — Network Engineering Internship

Complexe Haddadin, Oran · September 2025

By Brahmi Sarah Kaouther · 3-week immersion in carrier-grade network infrastructure

TL;DR: Completed a 3-week internship at Algérie Télécom's Complexe Haddadin in Oran, focusing on backbone network infrastructure (MPLS, BGP), CGNAT operations, IPv6 transition strategies, and Huawei OM network monitoring. Gained hands-on exposure to carrier-grade network security and critical infrastructure protection.

Overview

This internship at Algérie Télécom, Algeria's national telecommunications operator, provided an immersive experience into the operation and management of a large-scale carrier network. Based at the Complexe Haddadin in Oran, I worked alongside network engineers and security specialists, gaining practical exposure to the technologies that power Algeria's digital infrastructure.

Duration: September 1–21, 2025 (3 weeks)
Supervisor: Mme. Negad Djamila
Department: OSS (Operations Support System) — SIEM & Network Monitoring

Key Technologies Explored

1. Backbone Infrastructure — MPLS & BGP

The Algerian backbone network is a mesh of PE (Provider Edge) and P (Core) routers running MPLS (Multiprotocol Label Switching) for efficient packet forwarding and BGP (Border Gateway Protocol) for inter-domain routing. I observed how the Oran region's infrastructure connects to the national backbone and how traffic is routed between regions.

2. CGNAT (Carrier-Grade NAT)

CGNAT is deployed to address the IPv4 address shortage, allowing multiple subscribers to share a single public IPv4 address. This solution is critical for connectivity but introduces security challenges:

  • Reduced traceability: Multiple users sharing one public IP makes forensic investigations harder
  • Compatibility issues: Some services (VPNs, P2P applications) don't work correctly behind CGNAT
  • Monitoring complexity: Detailed port-based logging is required

3. IPv6 Transition

Algeria is gradually transitioning to IPv6 in dual-stack mode (both IPv4 and IPv6 coexist). This transition brings significant cybersecurity benefits:

  • End-to-end traceability (unique public addresses per device)
  • Native IPsec for authentication and encryption
  • Elimination of NAT, reducing attack surfaces
  • Resistance to mass scanning due to the vast address space

4. OM (Operation Management) Solution — Huawei

The Huawei OM platform is the central monitoring and management system for Algérie Télécom's network. It integrates several modules:

Module Function
FM (Fault Management) Detects anomalies and generates alarms
AM (Alarm Management) Collects and classifies alarms by severity (minor, major, critical)
IM (Inventory Management) Tracks all network equipment and configurations
SDM (Subscriber Data Management) Manages subscriber profiles, authentication, and access policies
BSS (Business Support System) Handles billing, order management, and customer relationship management
WFM (Workforce Management) Coordinates field technician assignments and task scheduling

5. Data Center Security — Oued Tillet

The Oued Tillet data center is a modern facility with 50+ cabinets and dozens of servers. Security is reinforced by:

  • BGP routing for optimized traffic flow
  • Two Fortinet firewalls in active-passive configuration (master-slave)
  • Packet filtering: normal packets are forwarded, suspicious packets are blocked
  • FortiGate security appliances for advanced threat protection

Key Takeaways

  • Carrier-grade networking requires high availability, redundancy, and fault tolerance
  • CGNAT is a necessary compromise but introduces security trade-offs
  • IPv6 adoption is a strategic priority for long-term scalability and security
  • Network monitoring platforms (like Huawei OM) are the backbone of operations
  • Data center security combines network-level controls (firewalls, routing) with physical infrastructure protection

Conclusion

This internship provided invaluable insight into the operation of a national telecommunications infrastructure. I gained hands-on exposure to MPLS, BGP, CGNAT, IPv6, and enterprise-grade monitoring solutions. The experience also reinforced the importance of cybersecurity in protecting critical infrastructure — a perspective that will shape my future career in network and security engineering.

I'm grateful to the Algérie Télécom team at Complexe Haddadin for their mentorship and the opportunity to contribute to such a vital national infrastructure.
